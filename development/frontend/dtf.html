What is Direct to Film (DTF) Printing?

Direct to Film (DTF) is a revolutionary digital printing technology that allows designs to be printed onto a special PET film, which is then transferred onto a wide variety of fabrics and materials using a heat press. Unlike DTG (Direct to Garment), DTF doesn't require pre-treatment and works exceptionally well on both light and dark fabrics, synthetic and natural fibers.

1

Design Printing

Design printed in reverse onto PET film using DTF-specific inks (CMYK + White)

2

Adhesive Powder

Special hot-melt adhesive powder applied to printed design while ink is wet

3

Curing

Film cured using heat press, melting powder into adhesive layer

4

Transfer

Transfer pressed onto garment at 290-320°F for 10-20 seconds

5

Peel & Finish

Film peeled away leaving design permanently bonded to material

Why Choose DTF?

Universal Fabric Compatibility

Works on cotton, polyester, blends, canvas, nylon, and even leather

No Pretreatment Needed

Unlike DTG, DTF skips pre-treatment process, saving time and labor

Vivid Colors + Strong Opacity

White ink base allows designs to pop on dark or colored garments

Exceptional Durability

Transfers are wash-resistant, flexible, and long-lasting

On-Demand Production

Perfect for short runs, custom orders, and quick turnaround

DTF vs Other Printing Methods

✅ DTF Advantages

  • • Works on ANY fabric type
  • • No pretreatment required
  • • Excellent on dark garments
  • • Store transfers for later use
  • • Soft but durable feel
  • • Full-color complexity
  • • Fast turnaround times

⚠️ Considerations

  • • Slightly thicker feel than DTG
  • • More noticeable than sublimation
  • • Requires proper curing process
  • • Not ideal for massive bulk runs
  • • Film handling requires care
