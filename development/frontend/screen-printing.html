What is Screen Printing?

Screen printing (also known as silkscreen printing or serigraphy) is a traditional printing technique where ink is pushed through a mesh screen that's been prepared with a stencil of the design. Each color in the design requires its own screen, and the ink is layered one color at a time onto the substrate—usually fabric, but also paper, wood, metal, and more. It's one of the most widely used and reliable methods for printing apparel and promotional goods due to its durability, vibrancy, and scalability.

1

Artwork Separation

Design separated by color. Each color requires its own film and screen

2

Screen Preparation

Mesh screens coated with light-sensitive emulsion and exposed to UV light

3

Setup & Registration

Screens aligned on printing press to ensure proper registration between colors

4

Printing Process

Ink pushed through stencil using squeegee. Each color printed separately

5

Curing & Finishing

Garment goes through conveyor dryer to cure ink ensuring durability

Why Choose Screen Printing?

Unmatched Durability

Thick inks (especially plastisol) are long-lasting and wash-resistant

Vibrant, Opaque Colors

Prints stay bold and clean, even on dark garments

Cost-Efficient for Bulk Orders

Lower cost per unit due to screen reuse - ideal for large quantities

Specialty Ink Versatility

Puff, metallic, glow-in-the-dark, glitter, suede, high-density inks available

Bold Graphic Impact

Perfect for layering textures and creating striking visual effects
